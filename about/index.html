About Me

I'm Success Ikechukwu, a web developer who enjoys building things that make sense in the real world—whether it’s a responsive e-market, a solar calculator, or a clean product page that just works. I focus on combining technical skills with practical thinking, creating digital tools that are useful, scalable, and user-friendly.

My recent work includes Zama-Stores, an online mall with dynamic cart features, JSON-based product organization, and a delivery service layer. I build using technologies like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Tailwind, Bootstrap, React.js, and Git/GitHub for version control. I also spend time customizing themes, designing intuitive UIs, and ensuring compatibility with both light and dark modes. Beyond frontend development, I'm exploring Python for data science and automation. I enjoy tackling challenges that involve real-life logic—like building systems to calculate compound interest or analyzing voter data trends.
